### Science — Magnesium Forms Deep Dive

**Lead Finding:** The evidence has bifurcated — Glycinate wins for sleep/anxiety; Threonate wins for cognition.

- **Magnesium Glycinate (Bisglycinate):** A 2025 RCT in *Nutrients* (155 adults, poor sleep) showed 28% improvement on the Insomnia Severity Index vs. 18% for placebo (p=0.049). A 2024 systematic review in *Cureus* concluded magnesium is "likely useful in the treatment of mild anxiety and insomnia." ⚠️ Limited evidence specifically on glycinate for anxiety/depression — commonly used but more robust studies needed.
- **Magnesium L-Threonate (Magtein®):** Only form clinically shown to cross the blood-brain barrier. 2024 RCT (*Sleep Medicine X*): significantly outperformed placebo for deep sleep, REM sleep, and readiness scores. 2025 RCT (*Frontiers in Nutrition*, 100 adults 18–45): improves memory and reduces "brain age" by 7.5 years at 2g/day.
- **Magnesium Citrate:** High bioavailability but natural osmotic laxative effect — not appropriate for a capsule product targeting sleep and wellness.
- **Perimenopause relevance:** Glycinate best addresses the most common complaints (insomnia, anxiety, muscle tension). If brain fog is primary, threonate is stronger.
- **Dosing ceiling:** NIH tolerable upper intake for supplemental magnesium is 350mg/day.

**Formula Recommendation:** Stick with magnesium glycinate (200–300mg elemental) as primary form. Seriously evaluate a "dual-form" upgrade: add Magtein® L-threonate (500–1,000mg compound = ~38–75mg elemental) alongside glycinate to legitimately claim both sleep *and* cognitive/mood support. The 2025 Frontiers in Nutrition RCT enrolled adults 18–45, directly applicable to your customer profile.

### Science — Dosing Optimization

**Lead Finding:** 3g/day is likely the floor for muscle saturation, but probably insufficient for cognitive effects in healthy young adults. The dose-benefit relationship for cognition is bifurcated: high (≥5g) for brain, low (3g) for muscle maintenance.

- **What does 3g actually do?** ~1–3g/day replaces normal daily losses. Supplementation can augment intramuscular creatine saturation by ~20%. At 1.5–3g/day, the formula supports this incremental gain — but don't overclaim.
- **Cognitive claims at low doses — thin ice:** The only RCT at ~2.2g/day found no significant differences between creatine and placebo in any cognitive endpoint. EFSA (2024) reviewed a formal health claim for "creatine → cognitive function" at 3g/day and found no effect.
- **Higher doses for brain:** Dosages of 20g+ have shown the most consistent results for increasing brain creatine levels. Only ~5% of total body creatine is stored in the brain.
- **Where cognitive science does support a signal:** A 2024 meta-analysis (16 RCTs) found significant positive effects on memory, attention time, and processing speed. Creatine was **more beneficial in females and those aged 18–60 years.** Effects most pronounced under conditions of metabolic stress or fatigue.
- **⚠️ Flag:** The brain-benefit literature is dominated by 5–20g doses, not 1.5–3g. Muscle energy support is the scientifically defensible claim at your range.

**Formula Recommendation:** Land on 3g creatine monohydrate — minimum credible dose with saturation evidence. Pair with "supports cellular energy and muscle recovery" structure/function claim. At 3g creatine + 200mg magnesium glycinate, you can fit the full formula into **4 capsules** at ~800mg each.

### Science — FDA Claims & Regulatory

**Lead Finding:** FDA is in a "New Era" of proactive enforcement — actively monitoring all channels including social media, websites, and influencer content.

- **Structure/function claims — three hard requirements per FDA (21 CFR 101.93):** (1) Must have substantiation *before* making claims. (2) Must notify FDA within 30 days of first marketing. (3) Label must include the DSHEA disclaimer ("FDA has not evaluated...").
- **Dec 2025 — Disclaimer Placement Relief:** FDA will no longer require the disclaimer on every label panel where a claim is made, as long as it appears on the label and is clearly linked.

| ❌ Danger Zone | ✅ Safe Language |
|---|---|
| "Clinically proven" | "Supported by research" |
| "Improves memory" | "Supports normal cognitive function" |
| "Treats fatigue" | "Supports energy metabolism" |
| "Reduces anxiety" | "Supports a calm mood" |
| "Freshest = more potent" | "Made to order for optimal freshness" |

- **⚠️ Flag for Ross:** The "freshness = higher efficacy" angle borders on an implied efficacy/superiority claim. Stick to *process* messaging ("made to order") rather than *outcome* messaging ("works better because it's fresh").
- **⚠️ Flag — EFSA Cognition Warning:** Health claims linking creatine and cognitive function improvement are not scientifically supported per EU EFSA at continuous low doses. In the U.S., "supports brain energy metabolism" is safer than "improves cognitive performance."

**Formula Recommendation:** File structure/function claims with FDA within 30 days of launch. Pre-cleared claims: (1) **"Supports cellular energy production"** (creatine/ATP, well-substantiated), (2) **"Supports muscle relaxation and restful sleep"** (magnesium, well-substantiated). Never use "clinically proven," "more effective," or "stronger formula."

### Science — Women's Health & Creatine

**Lead Finding:** The evidence is real, but skewed toward post-menopause — new 2025 RCT fills a critical gap for perimenopause specifically.

- **Cognition & Brain Fog:** 2025 RCT (CONCRET-MENOPA): 1,500 mg/day creatine HCl improved reaction time (1.2 vs. 6.6%; p<0.01) and raised frontal brain creatine levels in perimenopausal and menopausal women. 2024 meta-analysis (16 RCTs): significant positive effects on memory, attention, processing speed — **more beneficial in females.**
- **Mood & Depression:** Dietary creatine intake is inversely proportional to depression occurrence (31% greater incidence in lowest quartile). Depression rates 2x higher in females. CONCRET-MENOPA trial showed potential advantage in **reducing severity of mood swings** (p=0.06).
- **⚠️ Bone Density FLAG:** A 2-year double-blind RCT with 200 postmenopausal women at 3g/day found creatine supplementation **did not improve bone health** without exercise. Do NOT make bone density claims without tying them to an exercise program.
- **Sleep:** A 2025 quasi-experimental study found perimenopausal women showed positive improvements in sleep quality (p=0.0181).
- **Research Gap:** As of May 2025, there had been no studies examining creatine in perimenopausal women specifically. CONCRET-MENOPA (August 2025) is the first. You are launching with fresh, validating science.

**Formula Recommendation:** Lock in 1.5g creatine monohydrate as your minimum clinically defensible dose — CONCRET-MENOPA shows 1,500 mg/day raises frontal brain creatine levels and improves reaction time in perimenopausal women. This allows a 3g dose in just 3 capsules (paired with magnesium glycinate), staying within your 4–6 capsule ceiling. Position cognitive and mood angle ("mental clarity during hormonal transitions") as primary claim.
